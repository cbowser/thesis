Street View

overview

While gathering imagery and location data for Google's Street View project, it was found that Google was also collecting private data. This included email and text messages, passwords, internet usage history, and other "highly sensitive information."

denial

When an initial investigation was conducted in 2010, Google denied collecting private data. However, eventually Google admitted that this data collection was a deliberate decision by one of their software designers.

result

After placing the blame on a single unnamed engineer, refusing to hand over the data collected, and admitting to the collection of private data, Google, a multibillion dollar company, was made to pay a $25,000 fine.
